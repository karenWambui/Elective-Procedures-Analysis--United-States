**Executive Summary**
I used Power BI to create this project to analyze elective procedures across health service areas in the United States to understand discharge patterns,
length of stay (LOS), and cost drivers.Using hospital discharge data, the analysis identifies how patient severity, mortality risk, procedure type, 
and geography impact hospital utilization and cost efficiency.
The findings reveal that hip replacement is the most common elective procedure,New York City has the highest hospital concentration, and higher severity 
and mortality risk significantly increase LOS and cost.
These insights can support hospital administrators in optimizing capacity, managing costs, and improving patient flow.

**Business Problem**
Healthcare management sytems need to balance quality of care with operational efficiency and cost control.
Variability in patient severity,facility practices, and regional healthcare delivery makes it difficult to understand;
1.Why some hospitals have longer stays and higher costs than others
2.Which factors most strongly influence LOS and cost per discharge
3.Where opportunities exist to improve efficiency without compromising care.
The business problem was to identify key drivers of LOS and cost and highlight patterns that can inform better decison makin in regards to operational decisions.
